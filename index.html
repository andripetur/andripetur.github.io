About

A 24 year old Icelandic musician, living in the Netherlands with his fiancée. He studies klank ontwerp at the Utrecht university of the arts. His interests include music, literature and programming.

Andri begun his journey in music when he started taking violin lessons at the ripe age of 9, with the intention of becoming the next Mozart. At 12 years old he started to teach himself to play guitar and decided it would be more appropriate in this day and age to become the next Kurt Cobain. But after dabbling with heavy metal and having overcome most of his teenage angst, he considered that the british invasion would be a more appropriate, and took after general Lennon and co. Followed by the discovery of beats and bohemians, adapting ever increasingly deep lyrics and big brimmed hat, because Bob Dylan had it right all along. But then he saw the light and discovered drum machines, synthesizers, DAW's and the undepletable energy of LCD Soundsystem, maybe after all electronic music was the way to go? And somewhere in between these waves of mut(il)ation he also managed to get over excited by Eminem, Miles Davis, Howling Wolf & The Bad Plus alongside countless of other trends.
Having gone through the works of music styles he managed (or so he chooses to believe), to come out as a whole person on the other end with a love for all styles and genres. He now hand picks his inspiration from each style as he sees fit. The do it yourself attitude of punk, the fantasy filled lyrics of Cave & Waits, the groove of blues and the extravaganza of disco.
With a less is more attitude towards productions, he hopes to to inspire with his music, like he continues to be inspired by his heroes and idols.

(Ó)regla

(ó)regla is a live coding environment. Intended do showcase the power of an algorithmic approach to composition, utilizing chaotic systems to generate melodic content, and Schillingers approach to generating rhythms. Through interface you can generate, modify and sequence sound and actions.

  • Date: June 2016
  • Project website: (ó)regla

Gosi

Gosi is a Icelandic duet that consist of music tech Andri Pétur and singer Marta Sif. The couple makes indie-dance music, inspired by all music you can dance to. Disco, punk, country, funk, and everything in between. Wielding hypnotising rhythms, magical visuals and quirky melodies, they sing their way into every dance-lovers heart.

The band formed in 2012 when Andri discovered the magic of electronic music, and the ability to dance. Marta quickly joined in, providing vocals, harmonies and gogo dancing as needed, and they have been busy with the project ever since.

formRof

formRof is a program designed to enhance a live electronic performance. Using data from real time music analysis, as well as the performers via a Kinect sensor to generate visuals. Made to be used during live performances of Gosi, to make a spectacle of the musicians as well as the music.

Visuals are programmed in c++ using openFrameworks, and music analysis is done with max. Input data is for example mapped to movement, colours and scene changes of the video.

  • Client: Gosi
  • Date: January 2015
  • Service: Visuals

Hávaðamál

Hávaðamál is a performance of an algorithmic audio/visual composition. It is based on the text of Hávamál, an old icelandic poem that tells the story when Odin visited middle-earth and gave advice on how to live life. The bro code of the vikings.

A few interpretations of the text are layered to create a whole composition. The centerpiece is the traditional half-read/half-sung way of reciting the poem. From every verse a few words are paired with a hand picked audio sample, or video clip; which are then playedback and manipulated using an algorithmic analysis of the text.

The performance was premiered during E-live in the Nicolaikerk Utrecht on February 3 2017.

  • Date: February 2017
  • tags: Audio/visual composition

NIMpho

Feedback is a recurring theme in my work. There is something tantalizing about plugging a system into itself and dance on the edge of chaos and total meltdown.

NIMpho is an exploration and a celebration of continous feedback. NIM is an acronym for no input mixing and pho indicates the soupy mixture of sound and video.

  • Service: Improvisation, performance, noise

MGNT

MGNT is an interactive audio-visual installation in the form of a large circular tape (10m diameter). MGNT acts as a large sensor. With microphones and a Kinect, it hears and sees the visitors in the installation. Reacting to their movements & sounds with its own sounds and visusals.
